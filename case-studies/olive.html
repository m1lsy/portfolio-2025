Rapid Development Sprints:
Olive & Folio

Role

UX Designer & Flutter Developer

Timeline

Two 3-week sprints, 2025

Tools

Flutter, Dart, Figma, Git

Overview

These two mobile applications were developed during intense three-week sprints, designed to challenge our ability to rapidly conceptualize, design, and implement functional apps while maintaining code quality. The compressed timeline pushed us to make quick decisions, prioritize features effectively, and adapt our usual development processes.

Sprint-Based Learning Approach

  • Rapid prototyping and decision-making
  • Focus on MVP (Minimum Viable Product)
  • Quick research and user testing cycles
  • Daily iterations and feedback loops
  • Efficient Git workflows and collaboration

Olive: Recipe Discovery App

Challenge & Context

As a student myself, I often struggle with deciding what to cook every day. Many students face similar challenges:

  • Decision fatigue when choosing meals
  • Difficulty finding quick, affordable, and nutritious recipes
  • Scattered recipe inspiration across various social platforms
  • Lack of organization for saved recipes
  • Incomplete recipe information on social media
How might we help students easily collect, save, and access meal inspiration through a mobile experience?

Folio: Portfolio Logging App

Challenge & Research

I was tasked with designing and developing an app that uses mobile phones to make portfolio creation easier and more intuitive for students in our study. The portfolio we are required to create differs from a traditional one, as it serves as comprehensive proof of our work throughout the semester to show to our assessors, documenting every step and process rather than simply showcasing our best pieces.
To better understand how students currently use their phones to assist with portfolio creation, documentation, and reflection, I conducted several interviews. The insights gathered revealed meaningful trends in how students approach portfolio work and engage in reflective practice:

  • Evidence is often stored in fragmented ways (screenshots, notes, folders)
  • Most documentation happens on laptops, but phones are used for quick captures
  • Re-writing notes into portfolios feels like repetitive work
  • Students want better tagging and filtering options

Key User Needs

  • Tagging system to connect evidence to projects and outcomes
  • Easy sync between phone and laptop
  • Reflection prompts and structured organization
  • Motivation to make daily logs about what work was covered during that day.
Students confirmed that tagging by learning outcomes at the moment of capture would save time and reduce procrastination later.

User Research: Olive Recipe App

Interview Insights

I conducted interviews with two university students to understand their cooking habits and recipe discovery processes:

  • Luca (23, Italy) – Cooks 4-5 times weekly, prioritizes affordable, flavorful meals
  • Veele (21, Netherlands) – Daily cook, focuses on healthy, low-effort meals

Key Findings

  1. Decision Fatigue: Students spend excessive time choosing meals, often defaulting to familiar options.
  2. Fragmented Discovery: Existing apps are paywalled or ad-heavy; social media recipes lack complete information.
  3. Speed Priority: 30-minute meals using available ingredients are preferred.
  4. Swipe Appeal: The "Tinder-for-recipes" concept received positive feedback for its playful, efficient approach.
  5. Visual Focus: Clear imagery and minimal text are essential for quick decision-making.

Design & Iteration Process

Design Evolution & System Development

Both apps went through extensive design iterations, focusing on user feedback and usability testing:

Olive Recipe Cards Evolution

Folio App Evolution

UI Improvements

Olive App

  • Gesture Zones: Refined swipe areas to prevent accidental interactions
  • Information Density: Few details on card at first look with comprehensive recipe info when card is selected
  • Visual Hierarchy: Evolved from text-heavy to image-focused cards
  • Interaction Feedback: Added subtle animations for swipe actions

Folio App

  • Quick Capture: Reduced steps for easier evidence logging
  • Smart Tagging: Added tagging option for easy filtering by learning outcome
  • Visual Feedback: Implemented progress indicators and success states
  • Accessibility: Enhanced contrast ratios and touch target sizes

Recipe App User Testing Insights

Conducted testing with 5 design students, leading to key improvements:

  • The colour palette is a bit too plain and lacks contrast
  • Most said the layout is clear and easy to navigate
  • The navigation between pages is good and the nav bar is fitting
  • Discovery and saved recipe pages should have a search bar to easily find specific recipes

Design Iteration & Collaboration

While working on this project, I had the opportunity to build upon my teammate's initial design work. Taking their foundation, I applied my UX/UI expertise to enhance the user experience:

Key Design Changes

  • Border Radius: Changes the border radius to go back to 0 when text to text to make it look more cohesive.
  • Button Position: Changed the position of the 'heart' button to the right to make for sense when swiping.
  • Typography: Refined text styles and spacing for better readability. Before the same font was used for the Olive logo and in the UI.
  • Colour Palette: Adjusted the colour palette to make it more cohesive

This collaborative iteration process helped create a more polished and user-friendly final product while maintaining the original concept's strengths. It showcases how different perspectives can enhance a design through thoughtful iteration.

Olive: Technical Architecture

The recipe app was built with Flutter, implementing a clean architecture pattern:

Core Features

  • Custom gesture detection system for Tinder-style recipe swiping
  • Offline-first architecture with local recipe caching
  • Filtering system (cuisine, cooking time, ingredients)
  • Optimized image loading and caching for smooth scrolling

Technical Stack

  • Flutter SDK with Material 3 design system
  • TheMealDB API integration using Dio for network requests
  • Hive NoSQL database for persistent storage
  • Provider pattern for state management
  • Get_it for dependency injection

Architecture Highlights

  • Domain-driven design with clear separation of concerns
  • Repository pattern for data abstraction
  • Custom animation controllers for gesture feedback
  • Reactive UI updates using ChangeNotifier

Folio Portfolio App

Learning Through Constraints

The three-week timeline for this project pushed me to adapt my typically thorough research and iteration process into a focused sprint. This constraint became a valuable learning opportunity:

  • Practiced rapid decision-making and prioritization of features
  • Learned to conduct efficient user research with a smaller but focused participant group
  • Developed skills in quick prototyping and iterative feedback loops
  • Gained experience in adapting design processes to tight deadlines while maintaining quality

This experience taught me how to be more adaptable and efficient in my design process while still delivering meaningful solutions. The compressed timeline helped me develop a more streamlined approach to user research and iteration, skills that will be valuable in future professional projects.
