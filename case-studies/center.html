Center Browser Extension

Client

Studio Krom, Eindhoven

Role

UX Research, Development

Recognition

Best Digital Accessibility Concept

Overview

In this project, my team and I collaborated with our client Studio Krom, an innovative design and technology studio based in Eindhoven, to help make the digital world more accessible and inclusive.

Challenge

Accessibility is a broad and multifaceted topic, offering countless directions to explore. As a team, we quickly realized that trying to tackle the entire umbrella of accessibility would be overwhelming and difficult. We wanted to narrow it down to an aspect that felt personally relevant and potentially overlooked in mainstream conversations.

This led us to focus on ADHD - a condition that affects many people in our age group and immediate circles. Despite its prevalence, ADHD is often underrepresented in discussions around digital accessibility, making it a meaningful and impactful area for us to explore together with Studio Krom.

Research Process

To kick off our project, we conducted library research alongside a series of interviews with individuals who have ADHD. Our goal was to gain a deeper understanding of their experiences and the challenges they face—especially in relation to digital accessibility.

Research Challenges

To find participants for our interviews, I reached out to several sources:

  • Contacted Student+ at Fontys (limited by privacy policies)
  • Posted in relevant Reddit forums (removed by moderators)
  • Reached out to ADHD Café in Eindhoven

Despite these setbacks, I was still able to connect with individuals from my own network who were open to sharing their experiences.

"If I see everything on the screen at once, it's an overload—I start bouncing from one thing to the next. But if I can choose what I want to see, I can focus better. Out of sight, out of mind works both ways for me." — Individual with ADHD

Key Findings

Through my research, I discovered that individuals with ADHD often experience:

  • Heightened sensitivity to visual stimuli
  • Feelings of overwhelm in cluttered digital environments
  • Development of personal coping mechanisms
  • Need for specialized tools for organization and focus

Development

For the development phase, we focused on quality over quantity, choosing to implement two core features thoroughly rather than multiple features superficially. I worked with my teammate Yurick on the timer feature, including both styling and functionality.

Key technical challenges included:

  • First experience with React framework
  • Collaborative development workflows
  • Git version control
  • Browser extension architecture

The final implementation included:

  • Functional stopwatch component
  • Pomodoro timer with customizable settings
  • AI-powered text summarizer
  • User preference settings

Recognition & Outcome

The project culminated in winning the award for Best Digital Accessibility Concept during the final demo presentations.

This success was driven by:

  • Strong team collaboration with minimal conflicts
  • Excellent client relationship with Studio Krom
  • Regular feedback sessions and iterations
  • Focus on quality over feature quantity

This project was particularly meaningful as it created a tool that continues to be useful beyond the academic context. The extension successfully helps users with ADHD navigate digital spaces more effectively while maintaining focus and productivity.

The Center extension demonstrates how thoughtful design and focused features can make a real difference in digital accessibility. — Studio Krom
